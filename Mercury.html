Over view (Mercury)

Mercury, the smallest planet in our solar system, is slightly larger than Earth's Moon and covered in numerous impact craters. From its surface, the Sun appears over three times larger and sunlight is up to 11 times brighter. Despite being closest to the Sun, it's not the hottest planet; that distinction goes to Venus. However, Mercury earns its name as the fastest planet, completing an orbit around the Sun in just 88 Earth days.

Magnetic field strength:

Problems look mighty small from 150 miles up

The magnetic field is about 1% as strong as Earth's. Mercury's magnetic field is offset from its equator and interacts with the solar wind, creating intense magnetic tornadoes that send ions crashing into the surface and launching neutral atoms high into the atmosphere.

Volcanic activity:

Mercury has cooled to the extent that its temperature is no longer sufficient to melt rock, distinguishing it from larger planets such as Earth and Venus which still retain their high temperatures and exhibit ongoing volcanic activity

Rotation and orbit:

Mercury has an eccentric orbit, ranging from 29 million to 43 million miles from the Sun, and it orbits the Sun every 88 days at a speed of 29 miles per second. Its slow axial rotation of 59 Earth days creates a unique phenomenon where the Sun appears to rise, set, and rise again during certain parts of the planet's surface, making a full day-night cycle on Mercury last 176 Earth days.

Rings: Mercury doesn't have rings.

Surface:

Mercury resembles Earth's Moon with its crater-covered surface, lacking significant geological activity or atmosphere. The unexplored planet's mysterious surface includes perpetually shaded crater sections that scientists speculate may contain frozen water.

Potential for life:

Mercury's extreme temperatures and intense solar radiation make it an inhospitable environment for life as we know it, with conditions that are unlikely to be suitable for organisms to adapt and survive.

Geologic activity:

Similar to Earth, Mercury possesses a molten core. As this core gradually cools, its increased density causes slight contraction, leading to the collapse of the rocky outer crust and the formation of scarps on the planet's surface.

Atmosphere

Mercury lacks a substantial atmosphere but has a tenuous exosphere comprising atoms ejected from the surface by the solar wind and meteoroid impacts. Dominated by oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, and potassium, this thin exosphere characterizes the planet's atmospheric composition.
