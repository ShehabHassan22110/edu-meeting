1.Content of Pre-travel training

- Overview of the experience and what to expect - Safety procedures and emergency protocols specific to the VR or zero gravity environment - Physical training exercises to prepare for the physical demands of the experience, such as balance and coordination exercises - Educational content related to space exploration, astronomy, or other relevant topics - Equipment and technology training, such as how to use VR headsets or operate zero gravity simulators

2. Length of Pre-travel training

- The length of pre-travel training will depend on the complexity and duration of the experience, as well as the physical requirements of the customer. Here are some general guidelines: - For VR experiences, pre-travel training may be shorter, lasting a few hours or a day, and focus more on equipment and technology training than physical preparation. - For zero gravity flights, pre-travel training may last several days to a week to ensure that customers are physically fit and understand the safety procedures specific to the zero gravity environment.

3.What you can bring with you on your trip with us ?

- Prescription medication: Travelers may bring prescription medication on board, as long as it is properly labeled and stored. It is important to inform our medical team of any medication needs prior to the spaceflight. - Personal hygiene items: Travelers may bring personal hygiene items, such as a small amount of lotion or lip balm. - Comfort items: Travelers may bring comfort items, such as a small stuffed animal or a favorite book, as long as they do not pose a safety risk and do not take up too much space. - For zero gravity flights, customers may need to wear comfortable clothing and secure any loose items to prevent them from floating away during the flight. - For educational experiences, customers may need to bring notebooks, cameras, or other equipment to document their learning and experiences. - Foods & Drinks

4.What you can't bring with you on your trip with us ?

Personal electronics: Customers may be allowed to bring personal electronics such as phones or cameras to document their experience, but they should be advised to use them responsibly and not to interfere with the VR equipment or the gravity experience. Flammable items: Any item that is flammable, including lighters, matches, and flammable liquids, is strictly prohibited. These items pose a significant fire risk in the confined space of the bus. Sharp objects: Any item that is sharp or pointed, such as scissors or needles, may not be allowed on the board due to the potential for injury. Large bags or luggage: Customers should be advised to avoid bringing large bags or luggage on board the bus, as space may be limited. Valuable items: It is recommended not to bring valuable items or large sums of money on board the bus. There may be limited storage space and it is important to prioritize safety and minimize the risk of loss or damage.
